Like many households, we started with a sophisticated grocery management system built on years of careful planning and research.

We created a WhatsApp group.

At first, it seemed perfectly reasonable. Everybody already had WhatsApp installed. Everybody knew how to use it. Why introduce another app when the entire world has collectively decided that WhatsApp is the answer to every communication problem, regardless of whether it actually is?

Need to coordinate dinner plans? WhatsApp.

Need to share vacation photos? WhatsApp.

Need to discuss home renovations? WhatsApp.

Need to maintain a structured list of tasks that multiple people can edit simultaneously? Well… apparently WhatsApp.

## Conversations Are Not Lists

The cracks started showing surprisingly quickly.

The fundamental problem is that conversations and lists are completely different things. Conversations are chronological. Lists are stateful. A conversation is a record of what happened. A grocery list is a representation of what still needs to happen.

Those sound similar until you’ve spent ten minutes scrolling through messages trying to determine whether somebody already bought cheese.

At some point your grocery list starts looking less like a shopping plan and more like the transcript of a confused hostage negotiation.

Somebody adds milk.

Somebody else says they already bought milk yesterday.

A third person mentions that we’re also running low on coffee.

Then the conversation somehow transitions into discussing weekend plans, a broken vacuum cleaner and whether the weather forecast can be trusted.

Three days later you’re standing in a supermarket trying to reconstruct reality from a message thread that has accumulated forty-seven unrelated updates.

## We Accidentally Turned WhatsApp Into Everything

The beauty of WhatsApp is that it excels at being a messaging app. The problem is that people keep trying to use it as everything else.

We’ve collectively turned it into a grocery list, a task manager, a file storage system, a project management platform and occasionally a substitute for formal documentation. Somewhere along the way we forgot that different tools exist because different problems exist.

Nobody would use a frying pan as a pillow and conclude that pillows are overrated.

Yet we do the software equivalent all the time.

## A Grocery List Should Remove Ambiguity

What’s interesting is that most household organization problems aren’t particularly complicated. They don’t require machine learning, predictive analytics or a dashboard with seventeen charts. They simply require a shared understanding of what still needs doing.

A grocery list isn’t valuable because it’s technologically impressive. It’s valuable because it removes ambiguity.

You need milk.

You buy milk.

The milk disappears from the list.

Civilization advances.

That’s the entire workflow.

The moment you introduce a conversation into that process, things become significantly more chaotic. Conversations create context. Lists eliminate context. One of those is useful when planning a vacation. The other is useful when trying to remember whether you’re out of dishwasher tablets.

## Sometimes Boring Software Is Correct

In retrospect, using WhatsApp as a grocery list wasn’t a terrible idea.

It was actually a great reminder that software categories exist for a reason.

Sometimes the boring solution is boring because it works.
