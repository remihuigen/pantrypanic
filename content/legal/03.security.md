*Last updated: 20 June 2026*

Pantry Panic is a small app, but security still matters.

You trust Pantry Panic with household data: grocery lists, recipes, meal plans, account details, and the occasional “please buy toilet paper or society collapses” note. We take that seriously.

This page explains how we protect the hosted Pantry Panic service at **pantrypanic.app**.

## Hosted service only

This page applies to the hosted Pantry Panic service at **pantrypanic.app**.

It does not apply to self-hosted Pantry Panic installations. If you self-host Pantry Panic, you are responsible for securing your own server, Cloudflare account, secrets, database, storage, backups, and deployment setup.

That sounds like a lot because it is. Self-hosting is fun, but it is not magic.

## Infrastructure

The hosted Pantry Panic service runs on Cloudflare infrastructure.

We use:

- Cloudflare Workers for hosting and application runtime;
- Cloudflare D1 for database storage;
- Cloudflare R2 for file storage;
- Cloudflare KV for cache and supporting data;
- Cloudflare Turnstile for bot protection;
- Cloudflare Workers Logs for operational logging.

This keeps the infrastructure relatively small and boring. Boring is good. Boring means fewer weird moving parts waiting to ruin a Tuesday, or worse: Friday afternoon.

## Encryption

Pantry Panic uses HTTPS to encrypt data in transit between your browser and the hosted service.

Stored data is encrypted at rest by the infrastructure providers we use.

This includes database data, file storage, and other hosted infrastructure components.

## Passwords and sessions

We do not store plain-text passwords.

Passwords are stored as hashes. That means we cannot read your actual password, and neither should anyone else.

Pantry Panic also uses session tokens to keep you logged in. Session tokens should be treated like keys. If your device is compromised, your session may be compromised too.

## Access control

Pantry Panic separates data by household.

Users can only access household data for households they belong to. Household owners can manage members and remove access.

Administrative access to production systems is restricted to the people who need it to operate the service.

We do not inspect grocery lists, recipes, or meal plans unless it is necessary for a specific support, security, legal, or operational issue.

## Secrets

Production secrets are stored in dedicated secret management systems, including GitHub Secrets and Proton.

Secrets are not stored in the source code.

Because committing secrets to a repository is one of those mistakes that looks small until it becomes a full-body migraine. To prevent this, we use tooling for proactive secret scanning.

## Backups and recovery

Pantry Panic uses backups and recovery mechanisms to protect against technical failure and accidental data loss.

Database recovery data may be retained for up to 30 days. Additional weekly backups may be retained for up to 365 days.

Backups are used for recovery. They are not used for casual access, analytics, or curiosity.

## Logs

Pantry Panic uses operational logs to keep the service running, debug problems, and investigate security issues.

Logs may include technical data such as timestamps, request information, IP addresses, user agents, errors, and performance information.

Logs are not used for advertising, tracking, profiling, or selling you increasingly specific brands of cereal.

## Bot protection

Pantry Panic may use Cloudflare Turnstile to protect login, registration, invitation, or other sensitive flows from bots and abuse.

Turnstile helps keep automated junk away without turning every visit into a puzzle about crosswalks.

## What we do not do

We do not:

- sell personal data;
- use ad pixels;
- use Google Analytics;
- use session replay;
- profile users for marketing;
- inspect household data for growth insights;
- store plain-text passwords.

## Responsible disclosure

If you find a security issue in Pantry Panic, please report it.

Email:

[remihuigen@proton.me](mailto:remihuigen@proton.me)

Please include:

- a clear description of the issue;
- steps to reproduce it;
- the affected URL or feature;
- screenshots or proof of concept, if useful;
- your contact details if you want a response.

Please do not:

- access, change, or delete other users’ data;
- run destructive tests;
- perform denial-of-service attacks;
- publicly disclose the issue before we have had a reasonable chance to fix it;
- use the issue for anything other than responsible reporting.

In short: help us fix the hole, do not climb through it wearing muddy boots.

## Security limitations

No online service is perfectly secure.

We use reasonable technical and organisational measures to protect Pantry Panic, but we cannot guarantee that incidents will never happen.

If we discover a security incident that affects your personal data, we will investigate it and, where required, notify affected users and the relevant authorities.
