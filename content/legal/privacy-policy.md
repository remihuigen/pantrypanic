# Privacy Policy

Last updated: 19 June 2026

Pantry Panic is a grocery list app for households. It helps you manage lists, recipes, meal planning, and the tiny domestic chaos that somehow starts with “we’re out of milk”.

This Privacy Policy explains what personal data we collect, why we collect it, and what you can do about it.

We keep this as simple as possible. No ads. No tracking pixels. No selling your data. No creepy “we noticed you like oat milk” nonsense.

## What this policy applies to

This policy applies to the hosted Pantry Panic service available at:

**pantrypanic.app**

It does **not** apply to self-hosted Pantry Panic installations.

If you self-host Pantry Panic, your data stays with your own setup. We do not receive your app data, your household data, your grocery lists, or your recipes. You, or whoever runs the self-hosted instance, are responsible for that installation.

## What data we collect

We collect the data needed to make Pantry Panic work. That is the whole plan.

### Account data

When you create or use an account, we process:

- your email address;
- your display name;
- your password hash;
- session tokens;
- magic link or reset tokens, if you use login or reset links;
- your avatar, if you upload one;
- account creation date.

Your language and timezone settings stay in your browser.

We do not store your plain-text password. We store a hashed version, because storing actual passwords would be impressively stupid.

### Household data

Pantry Panic is built around households. We process:

- household names;
- household membership;
- household roles, such as member or household owner;
- household settings;
- invite links and reset links;
- who created or updated certain household data.

Household members can see the shared data inside their household. That is the point of a shared household app.

### Grocery, recipe, and meal-planning data

Depending on how you use Pantry Panic, we process:

- shopping lists;
- list names, icons, and order;
- grocery items;
- item categories;
- amounts, units, and notes;
- checked, archived, and deleted item states;
- recipes;
- recipe descriptions;
- recipe servings;
- recipe source URLs;
- recipe ingredients;
- meal planner days;
- meal planner placeholders and notes;
- created-by and updated-by information.

You decide what you put into Pantry Panic. Please do not store sensitive personal data in grocery items, recipe notes, or list names. Pantry Panic is for “eggs”, not your medical history.

### Payment data

If you buy a Pantry Panic lifetime deal, payments are handled by **Mollie**.

We may process:

- payment status;
- payment reference IDs;
- product or plan information;
- transaction timestamps;
- customer or payment IDs from Mollie, where needed;
- invoice or billing details, where required by law.

We do not process or store your full payment card details ourselves. That is handled by the payment provider.

### Technical and security data

When you use Pantry Panic, some technical data may be processed automatically by our hosting provider, Cloudflare.

This may include:

- IP address;
- request data;
- user agent;
- device or browser information;
- timestamps;
- error or performance logs;
- security events.

We use this data to keep the app running, prevent abuse, debug problems, and avoid the app falling over in a heap.

## What we do not do

We do **not**:

- show ads;
- use ad pixels;
- use Google Analytics;
- use Meta, TikTok, LinkedIn, or similar tracking pixels;
- use session replay;
- sell your data;
- profile you for marketing;
- inspect your shopping lists for fun, curiosity, or “growth insights”.

There is no growth-hacking goblin hiding behind the fridge.

## Why we process your data

We process your data for these reasons:

### To provide the service

We need account, household, list, recipe, and meal-planning data to run Pantry Panic.

Legal basis: performance of a contract.

### To keep the service secure

We use session data, access links, logs, and security checks to protect accounts and prevent abuse.

Legal basis: legitimate interest.

### To process payments

If you buy a paid plan or lifetime deal, we process the payment data needed to confirm and administer that purchase.

Legal basis: performance of a contract and legal obligation.

### To comply with the law

Some records may need to be kept for tax, accounting, legal, or dispute-resolution reasons.

Legal basis: legal obligation.

### To contact you about the service

We may send service emails, such as:

- login or magic links;
- account verification emails;
- password reset emails;
- household invitations;
- payment confirmations;
- important service updates.

Legal basis: performance of a contract and legitimate interest.

We do not send marketing emails unless we add that later and ask for permission where required.

## Cookies and local storage

Pantry Panic uses cookies and browser storage only where needed to make the app work.

This may include:

- keeping you logged in;
- storing session information;
- remembering local app preferences;
- protecting forms or login flows.

We do not use tracking cookies or advertising cookies.

## Bot protection

We may use **Cloudflare Turnstile** to protect parts of Pantry Panic against bots and abuse.

Turnstile helps us check whether a request is likely made by a human without turning the internet into a traffic-light-clicking simulator.

## Where your data is stored

Pantry Panic uses Cloudflare infrastructure, including:

- Cloudflare Workers for hosting;
- Cloudflare D1 for the database;
- Cloudflare R2 for file storage;
- Cloudflare KV for cache and supporting data;
- Cloudflare Workers Logs for operational logging.

Where supported, we configure primary app data storage in the European Union (D1 database, R2 Storage).

Some operational processing may still happen through our service providers. For example, hosting, email delivery, security checks, and payment processing may involve providers that process limited technical or account data outside your country. When that happens, we rely on the provider’s data protection terms and safeguards.

## How long we keep data

We keep data only as long as needed for the purpose it was collected.

### Account and household data

We keep account and household data while your account or household exists.

If you delete your account or household, we delete the related app data unless we need to keep some information for legal, security, payment, or dispute-resolution reasons.

### Deleted and archived app data

Pantry Panic supports archived and deleted states for app data, such as lists, recipes, and items. This helps the app behave predictably and prevents accidental data loss.

When data is permanently deleted, it may still remain in backups for a limited time.

### Backups

We use backups to recover from technical problems.

Database recovery data may be retained for up to 30 days. Additional weekly backups may be retained for up to 365 days.

Backups are not used to casually browse user data. They are there for recovery when something breaks. Ideally, they remain boring forever.

### Logs

Operational logs are kept for a short period. Cloudflare Workers Logs are generally retained for up to 7 days, depending on the plan and configuration.

### Payment and administration records

Payment and administration records are kept as long as required for accounting, tax, legal, and dispute-resolution purposes.

## Who can access your data

Your household data can be accessed by members of your household.

Household owners can manage household members, including removing members.

Pantry Panic administrators may have technical access to user data where needed to operate, secure, debug, or support the service. We do not inspect grocery lists or recipes unless it is necessary to handle a specific support, security, or legal issue.

## Children

Pantry Panic is designed to be household-friendly.

We do not intentionally collect sensitive data from children. Children may use Pantry Panic as part of a household, but account creation and household management should be handled by a parent, guardian, or responsible adult where required by law.

Please do not add sensitive information about children to grocery lists, recipe notes, or meal planner notes.

## Security

We take reasonable steps to protect your data.

This includes:

- HTTPS encryption in transit;
- encryption at rest for stored database data;
- restricted administrative access;
- secret management for production credentials;
- backups for recovery;
- bot protection where needed.

No system is perfectly secure. Anyone claiming otherwise is either lying or selling cybersecurity software.

If you find a security issue, please contact:

[remihuigen@proton.me](mailto:remihuigen@proton.me)

- access your personal data;
- correct inaccurate data;
- delete your data;
- restrict processing;
- object to processing;
- receive a copy of your data;
- withdraw consent, where processing is based on consent;
- complain to a privacy authority.

To make a request, email:

[remihuigen@proton.me](mailto:remihuigen@proton.me)

We may need to verify your identity before handling certain requests.

## Deleting your data

You can delete your account and households from the app where those features are available.

When you delete your account or household, we delete the related app data unless we need to keep certain records for legal, payment, security, backup, or dispute-resolution reasons.

Deleted data may remain in backups until those backups expire.

## Complaints

If you are in the European Union and you think we are not handling your data properly, you can contact us first.

You also have the right to complain to your local privacy authority.

In the Netherlands, this is the **Autoriteit Persoonsgegevens**.

## Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy when Pantry Panic changes, when our providers change, or when legal requirements change.

If the changes are important, we will try to make that clear in the app or by email.

Small wording changes may happen without dramatic announcements. Nobody needs a push notification because we fixed a comma.
