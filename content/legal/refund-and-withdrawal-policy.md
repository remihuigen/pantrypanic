# Refund and Withdrawal Policy

Last updated: 20 June 2026

This policy explains how refunds and withdrawal work for paid Pantry Panic purchases.

Pantry Panic gives every new user a free 30-day trial before payment. The trial includes the full product. Same app, same features, same grocery-list chaos. No watered-down demo version, no “upgrade to unlock carrots” nonsense.

Because of that, paid purchases are generally final after the trial ends, unless the law requires otherwise or something went wrong.

## Hosted service only

This policy applies to purchases for the hosted Pantry Panic service at **pantrypanic.app**.

It does not apply to self-hosted Pantry Panic installations. Self-hosted Pantry Panic is free for non-commercial use under the applicable license. Since you do not buy hosted access from us when you self-host, there is nothing for us to refund.

## Free 30-day trial

Every new Pantry Panic user gets a free 30-day trial of the hosted service.

During the trial, you can use the full product before deciding whether to buy paid access.

The trial is intended to give you enough time to find out whether Pantry Panic works for your household. Add lists, invite people, try recipes, plan meals, forget the one thing you went to the shop for anyway. The usual.

After the trial, you can choose whether to buy paid access.

## What you buy

Pantry Panic may offer paid lifetime deals, including:

- lifetime access for a single household;
- lifetime access for multiple households.

“Lifetime” means the lifetime of the Pantry Panic hosted service, not your biological lifetime, the lifetime of your fridge, or the heat death of the universe.

A lifetime deal is a one-time payment. It is not a subscription.

## Refunds after the trial

Because every user gets a free 30-day trial of the full product before payment, paid lifetime purchases are generally final.

We normally do not provide refunds simply because:

- you changed your mind after buying;
- your household stopped using Pantry Panic;
- you found another app;
- you forgot you had already tested the product during the trial.

That is not meant to be harsh. It is just the point of the trial: try first, pay later.

## When refunds may still apply

We may provide a refund if:

- the law requires it;
- you were charged incorrectly;
- your payment was duplicated by mistake;
- your paid access was not activated after payment;
- the paid service was not delivered;
- there is another serious issue and refunding is the fair thing to do.

Nothing in this policy limits mandatory consumer rights that apply to you.

## Withdrawal rights

If consumer law gives you a right of withdrawal or cancellation after purchase, we will respect that right.

Where required, we will provide the information, forms, or cancellation options needed to exercise that right.

If an exception applies to a legal withdrawal period, we will explain that before purchase where required.

- the email address used for your Pantry Panic account;
- the approximate purchase date;
- the payment reference, if you have it;
- a short explanation of what went wrong.

We may need to verify that the request comes from the person who made the purchase.

## Refund method

If a refund is approved, we will usually refund the payment through the original payment method.

Refunds are handled through our payment provider, Mollie.

The time it takes for the money to appear in your account may depend on your bank, card issuer, or payment method. Banks enjoy adding suspense to administration.

## After a refund

If your purchase is refunded, your paid access may be removed.

Your account may still exist unless you also delete it or ask us to delete it. Refunds and account deletion are separate things.

If you want your account or household data deleted, you can use the deletion options in the app where available, or contact us.

## Business purchases

Pantry Panic is mainly intended for consumers and households.

If you buy Pantry Panic for business, commercial, organisational, or professional use, consumer withdrawal rights may not apply.

We may still choose to offer a refund, but we are not required to treat business purchases the same as consumer purchases unless the law says otherwise.

## Local laws

This policy does not limit any mandatory rights you may have under the laws that apply to you.

If local consumer law gives you stronger rights than this policy, those rights still apply.

## Changes to this policy

We may update this policy when Pantry Panic changes, when payment options change, or when legal requirements change.

If the changes are important, we will try to make that clear in the app or by email.
